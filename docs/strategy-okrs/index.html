1. Winning aspiration

Proposed Own the UAE property transaction and the consumer relationship. End state: consumer AI layer within roughly three years. Mission is the contribution; aspiration is the win.

Immovable: owner and applicant outcomes always come first.

2. Where to play

Engage, the Property OS Brokers
  • Customer: brokers and brokerage management
  • Beachhead: Cencorp brokerages (BH, CRC, Linda's, Lomond)
  • Stepping stone, not the end state
  • Model: per-seat plus portal cost offset and services
Grace, the Deal Engine Standalone Jun 10
  • Customer: owner and applicant directly
  • Beachhead: overflow and dormant demand
  • Luxury excluded early (10M+ sales, 750k+ rentals)
  • Model: commission on AI-closed deals; Cencorp majority
Where we will not play
  • Full property-management CRM now (bridge via integration)
  • Generic chatbots serving every journey (Ask BetterHomes killed)
  • GCC expansion; UAE first, UK next
  • Commodity CRM feature bake-offs

3. How to win

Company integrative logic Proprietary UAE transaction-data and AI flywheel plus multi-actor network. White space: competitors chase brokers, not owners and applicants.
Engage OS — how to win
  • Make the broker more money: productivity plus referral network
  • Become the system a broker cannot close a deal without
  • Lock-in via data, workflow, company-owned conversation history
Grace — how to win
  • Close at a fraction of broker cost-to-serve
  • Own WhatsApp qualify-match-advise-book flow, minimal UI
  • Pick the appointment, not the property

The five moats

  • 25 years of transaction-linked data (being cleaned)
  • WhatsApp conversational data at scale
  • Transaction-linked listing removal
  • Seller sentiment data in valuation notes
  • ~800K scraping records, compounding daily
Honest caveat: the moat is unrealised May 4 meeting: after 2+ years, no clean datasets, no data science, lead scoring blocked. Binding constraint is hiring (data engineering, new CTO), not roadmap.
Reinforcement logic OS feeds data and distribution to Grace. Grace proves disintermediated economics. Both capture the same transaction layer. Hedge only holds if gated.

Open decisions, with recommendations

1. Aspiration: UAE transactions or broker seats?

Rick. Seats, services, portal offset as commercial fuel.

Ryan. Consumer AI layer; OS risks anchoring on brokers.

Recommendation. Aspire to share of UAE transactions. Seats/MRR fund stages 1–3, not the apex.

2. Cencorp-first or open-network-first?

Rick. Prove value, then widen.

Ryan. Alliance / MLS: non-signers don't get listings shown.

Recommendation. Dual-speed: mature OS on Cencorp; seed Alliance externally early.

3. Grace competes with Cencorp brokers, or only overflow?

Rick. Standalone AI-led agency; customer-first.

Ryan. Cautious; 6–9 months validation in BH first.

Recommendation. Decided Jun 10 as staged hedge. Pre-commit gates: Iteration 9, ≥3x economics, owner listing engagement. Protect Grace decision rights structurally.

4. How explicit can disintermediation be?

Rick. Bold; standalone decouples brand.

Ryan. Missionary not mercenary; publicly cautious.

Recommendation. Two narratives: OS = augmentation only; Grace (new name) = consumer-first. Entity split enforces this.
