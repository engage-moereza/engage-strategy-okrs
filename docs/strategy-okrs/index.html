Winning aspiration

Become the default operating and transaction layer for UAE real estate: the system brokerages run on, and the product consumers use to complete property journeys end to end.
Strategic belief Close information gaps to become indispensable. Portals own discovery; no one owns the full journey automatically as a product end to end.

Flywheel: Listings → Matches → Transactions → Market Knowledge → Listings.

How to win

Company logic Close information gaps across the full journey with AI, automation, and workflow transformation, plus a partner and referral network no single brokerage or portal matches alone. Compete on journey ownership, not broker tooling alone.
PropertyOS
  • Transform brokerage operations: CRM, deals, scorecards, automations, EngageAI for marketing and finance efficiency
  • Make brokers measurably more productive; referral network between firms
  • Lock-in via workflow, company-owned conversation history, and operational data brokers generate daily
Grace
  • Close deals at materially lower cost-to-serve than traditional brokerage
  • Own the consumer journey: qualify → match → advise → book viewing → (search, offer, close) on WhatsApp plus web where depth is needed
  • SmartView validated: applicants commit to viewings without a broker qualification call
Reinforcement logic (decided) PropertyOS on Cencorp; Grace as the external consumer bet. Eventually one model wins and absorbs the other. Until then: PropertyOS feeds some data and distribution; Grace builds independent supply (search, SmartList, owner direct). Gates below define when Grace expands scope.

OKRs · mid-cycle

Board-facing names unchanged: Acquire, Reheat, Retain, Refer, Moat (was Foundations).

One gradient, three KR types
  • Squad commit: what we control and ship. The only real promise.
  • Influence: the bridge. Earliest proof the commit is moving the outcome. Steered, not graded.
  • Customer outcome: the value to the customer. Rolls up into the company objectives (O1–O3).

Influence is not a separate level. It is the measurement bridge between squad commit and customer outcome. Pre-revenue, it is the only feedback loop.

OKR logic tree

Read bottom-up. Each squad commit should drive an influence signal, which should move a customer outcome, which rolls into a company objective, which advances the North Star. Solid = primary roll-up, dashed = secondary contribution. Critique the arrows.

Grace gates G1–G7 are a separate validation track and are deliberately not in this tree.

Critique prompts: where the logic is weakest
  • O1 Engine is overloaded: four of five objectives roll into it. Is it a genuine objective or a catch-all that means little?
  • O2 Sustainability has no primary owner: only dashed (secondary) lines reach it. Is revenue/sustainability an objective, or a cross-cutting constraint that shouldn't be an O-level item?
  • O3 Capabilities ≈ Moat (1:1): the company objective and the squad objective are the same thing. Collapse them, or is the company-vs-squad view worth the duplication?
  • Assumed arrows: which squad-commit → influence links are hopeful, not evidenced? e.g. SmartList → owned-applicant share, or Alliance v0 → internal-vs-external referral rate.
  • Missing rung: nothing in the tree connects directly to "transactions served well" except via revenue proxies. Is the North Star actually wired in, or decorative?

Objectives · detail

Acquire · Scale listing inventory and owned applicant traffic

Reduce portal dependency; grow inventory quality and organic demand.

Squad commit · control

  • Clients: Client Intelligence Engine live: intent scoring for ≥500 owners and ≥1,000 dormant applicants; ≥3 automated WhatsApp sequences in pilot
  • Clients: SmartList prototype live: owner onboarding to list via WhatsApp/web; engagement baselined on ≥200 owners
  • Brokers: Listing quality (four-P) checks instrumented in publish flow; manager approval gate live for pilot brokerage

Influence · bridge

  • Company-generated applicant share vs portal baseline (instrumented, tracked)
  • High-value inventory ratio in core areas (influence: depends on broker and management behavior)

Customer outcome · value

  • Lower net portal spend per listing for group brokerages via offset program
Reheat · Unlock dormant database value

High-margin deals from existing relationships without proportional broker manual effort.

Squad commit · control

  • Clients: ≥3 behavioral reactivation sequences live; open/reply rates baselined on first 1,000 contacts
  • Clients: ≥5% of contacts reached convert to qualified interaction in pilot

Influence · bridge

  • Reheat-sourced listings and deals tracked separately (no moonshot revenue target until baselines exist)

Customer outcome · value

  • Incremental revenue from database reactivation vs broadcast baseline
Retain · Qualification, conversion, and operational efficiency

Improve lead-to-deal efficiency; reduce cost-to-serve through AI and workflow.

Squad commit · control

  • Brokers: Deals & Scorecards live at BetterHomes; zero scorecard discrepancy 30 days; baselines confirmed ±5%
  • Brokers: EngageAI v1 live for ≥1 brokerage function (marketing or finance) with measured time saved
  • Clients: SmartView live at scale on WhatsApp; ≥200 applicants in pilot month 1; completion rate baselined
  • Clients: Applicant records 100% in Core Engage (migration complete)

Influence · bridge

  • Applicant-to-viewing conversion: SmartView cohort vs broker-handled control (influence)
  • First platform-attributed viewing bookings instrumented and counted (baseline year: none yet)

Customer outcome · value

  • Group brokerage cost-per-deal trend (context only: was BH-specific; reframe as group efficiency, not a deals/broker target)
Refer · Cross-firm and partner referral network

Reframed from Cencorp-internal LTV to Alliance / partner network across property services companies.

Squad commit · control

  • Brokers: Alliance Contract v0 drafted; ≥1 cross-firm referral logged and settled in pilot
  • Brokers: ≥1 partner referral prompt at a defined transaction moment (mortgage or conveyancing); baseline documented

Influence · bridge

  • Internal vs external referral rate tracked post-pilot
  • Referral GTV through Engage (influence: network liquidity)

Customer outcome · value

  • Cross-group and cross-firm revenue from referrals, not Cencorp capture alone
Moat · Data, capabilities, and delivery infrastructure

Was Foundations. The capabilities the strategy requires.

Squad commit · control

  • Engineering: Observability and CI/CD standardized; daily production releases as norm
  • Data: Property Monitor exit plan executed; DLD direct integration scoped; automated cleansing pipeline v1 live on one entity
  • Product: Opportunity-solution tree on all new initiatives; ≥1 weekly customer interview per squad
  • All: 100% team on AI tooling (Claude, Cursor); continuous discovery and continuous delivery cadence documented

Influence · bridge

  • Labeled conversion dataset toward 2,000+ (ML lead scoring threshold)
  • Rework rate ≤30% of delivery cycles

Customer outcome · value

  • Group efficiency thesis: automation and data infrastructure that compounds across brokerages
